# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

This project is an open academic work. We are committed to maintaining a welcoming and respectful environment for everyone who engages with it — regardless of experience level, background, institution, or perspective.

## Expected behaviour

- Be respectful in issues, pull requests, and any other communication related to this project.
- Engage with ideas rather than people. Disagreement about content is welcome; personal criticism is not.
- Acknowledge prior work and contributions accurately.
- Be direct and constructive. Clarity is more valuable than politeness that obscures meaning, but both matter.

## Unacceptable behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks in any form.
- Dismissing contributions based on the contributor's identity or institutional affiliation.
- Misrepresenting others' positions or work.
- Sustained disruption of discussion.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, and any other forum where contributors interact in connection with this project.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this code of conduct, please contact the project maintainers at the Centre for Change and Complexity in Learning (C3L), Adelaide University. Reports will be reviewed and responded to. We are committed to protecting the privacy of anyone who reports an issue.

## Enforcement

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject contributions, comments, or other interactions that do not align with this code of conduct, and to temporarily or permanently restrict contributors who demonstrate a pattern of unacceptable behaviour.
